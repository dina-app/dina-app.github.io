Beginner Friendly Guide

How to Draw Manga on a Magic Drawing Pad

New to digital manga? This page gives you a practical, step-by-step workflow you can use on a Magic Drawing Pad, with tips adapted from official drawing tutorials and the XP-Pen manual.

1) Set up your pad before you draw

  • Charge first: Start with a full battery so you can finish a sketch session without interruptions.
  • Use the included stylus directly: On XP-Pen Magic Drawing Pad, the pen is passive and does not need Bluetooth pairing.
  • Avoid accidental palm touches: Turn on stylus-priority settings in your app/device and use a drawing glove when needed.
  • Pick one app and stay consistent: MediBang Paint, ibis Paint X, and Clip Studio Paint are all common for manga workflows.

2) Follow the manga production pipeline (simple version)

A beginner-friendly manga flow is: Planning → Name/Storyboard → Rough → Inking → Fill blacks → Tone & finishing → Export. If you keep this order, your pages feel cleaner and faster to revise.

Planning

Write the scene goal in one line: “Who wants what in this page?” This keeps panels focused.

Name / Storyboard

Draw tiny panel thumbnails first (stick figures are fine). Decide speech bubble placement early.

Rough & Inking

Build rough pose lines, then ink with cleaner line weight: thicker outside silhouette, thinner inside detail.

Blacks, Tones, Effects

Use black fills for depth, then add screentones and effect lines to guide emotion and action.

3) Core drawing drills for manga style

  1. Face construction drill (10 min): Draw a circle + jaw, centerline, eye line, nose line, mouth line.
  2. Eye style drill (10 min): Practice 3 eye designs (soft, sharp, comedic) in sets of 6.
  3. Hair mass drill (10 min): Block big hair shapes first, then add strand accents only at the end.
  4. Pose rhythm drill (10 min): Draw gesture lines before anatomy to avoid stiff characters.
  5. Panel readability drill (10 min): Make one 4-panel page and ensure reading flow is clear at a zoomed-out view.

4) Screentone and panel tips that make pages look “manga”

  • Use halftone layers for clean, editable tones instead of flattening too early.
  • When placing materials/tones, select the panel first so textures stay inside panel borders.
  • Use transparent brush erasing on tone layers to create highlights in hair and clothing.
  • Check for moiré risk by reviewing tone density and previewing at export size before publishing.

5) Recommended 60-minute practice session

0–10 min

Warm-up lines: straight, curves, ellipses, taper strokes.

10–25 min

Character bust sketch + expression variations.

25–45 min

Ink final lines and place speech balloons.

45–60 min

Add blacks, simple tones, export, and write one lesson learned.

6) Common beginner mistakes (and quick fixes)

  • Stiff poses: Start from gesture line first, not body parts.
  • Messy pages: Keep one panel as the visual focus; simplify backgrounds elsewhere.
  • Flat depth: Push contrast with bigger black shapes behind the focal character.
  • Pen feels wrong: Recheck pressure curve in app settings and test with a default round brush.
